# Key findings

- The analysis targeted **degraded peatlands in South Sumatra** using a GIS–MCDA approach in ArcGIS Pro.
- Eight criteria were combined using **AHP-derived weights** and **weighted linear combination**.
- Protected areas and other restricted zones were treated as **hard constraints**.
- Within the eligible degraded peat analysis domain (**124,007.76 ha**):
  - **Highly suitable**: 66,665.25 ha (53.76%)
  - **Moderately suitable**: 30,867.84 ha (24.89%)
  - **Low**: 24,408.99 ha (19.69%)
  - **Very low**: 2,065.68 ha (1.67%)
- Overall, **78.65%** of eligible degraded peatland was classified as moderately suitable or highly suitable.
- The project is best framed as a **policy-oriented first-pass spatial screening tool** for agrivoltaic planning on degraded peatlands.
